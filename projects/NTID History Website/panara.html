Robert Panara, writer, poet, professor and pioneer of deaf studies

RIT's first deaf professor, hired in 1967 to help establish NTID on RIT's campus.

Panara was born in 1920 in The Bronx of New York City, and lost his hearing as a child from spinal meningitis. After high school, he attended and graduated from Gallaudet College (now Gallaudet University)

He went on to teach at Gallaudet College, before moving to the Rochester Institute of Technology where he helped found the National Technical Institute for the Deaf (NTID). He was the first deaf professor at RIT, and taught there for 30 years.
